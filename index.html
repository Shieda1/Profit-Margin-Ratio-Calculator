What is the profit margin?
profit margin is a profitability indicator that a company use to measure it's financial health. In simple words it is the after-tax profit generated by each sale, the number tells how profitable the business has been. It is also called the return on sales ratio.
How to calculate profit margin?
To calculate profit margin you should use the following formula:
profit margin = netincome / net sales
example: A company's sales are $2,000,000 and it's net income is $320,000, what is the profit margin of this company?
using the profit margin formula the answer will be:
profit margin = net income / net sales
profit margin = $320,000 / $2,000,000
profit margin = 16%
Why use profit margin?
profit margin i s important because it tells about the company's financial health and identify areas to improve. It is also used to benchmark against industry peers and bankers usually use it to evaluate the request of financing and the capacity to take on and repay debt.
